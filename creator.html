Race

There are dozens of races in world, each one bringing unique attributes, predispositions, values, cultures, and beliefs. Races are set apart from each other by physical strength and attributes, unique abilities, as well as more mundane things such as size and lifespan.

Attributes

Players start with a total of 30 attribute points to invest in the six attributes. Each point increases an attribute by +1. Each of the six attributes have a base starting value of â€“4 and may be increased to a maximum of +8 for a total of 12 points invested. This number is your Attribute Bonus (AB) and will affect your skill and specialization bonuses.

Archetype

Archetypes represent what a character has focused on in their free time since their last level. Since levels represent growth, characters typically only have time to develop in one major area: the art of war, self-improvement, or sinking deeper into the depths of arcana. Taking another level in an archetype lets you choose related abilities and increase relevant combat points.

Background

Past Life

No one grows up in a vacuum. The present is marked with the thumbprints of the past. Who were you before you became an adventurer? This next section will help you identify skills from your past. Nearly all adventurers come from humble beginnings. Choose a Past from the following table to represent skills you picked up before you took to a life on the road. They give minor bonuses to skills or introduce a specialization. Specializations represent specific study and esoteric knowledge which may be added to rolls involving knowledge from that specialization. These are exceptionally powerful, but are typically quite specific.

Social Circle

Social circle represents the type of people you grew up with or are used to interacting with. This should reasonably match with your chosen background or be approved by the loremaster. For example, you may have been a peasant, but on land owned by a monastery. You can choose between either Peasant or Clergy as your social circle, giving you benefits when interacting with that group. People within this group are more likely to share information and want to assist you should you need help.
Potential choices include, but are not limited to: Peasant, Middle-Class, Craftsmen, White-Collar/Artisan, Merchant, Nobility/Upper Class, Clergy, Animals, Military, Wizardry, Academia.

Regional Knowledge

You grew up in a region. You have knowledge of geography, history, and political forces of that area. This includes major landmarks, government structure and major political figures, recent and major historic events that affected the area, local folklore, common wildlife and fauna, and cities and towns and their relative geography to each other. You can recall this general information without a check, although specific or obscure details beyond this may require a successful skill check.
A region is usually about 40-100 miles square.
Alternate Rule: Nomadic. If you traveled as a lifestyle, then make relevant checks with advantage to gain basic information. However, you cannot check for details. The GM may give you info from an outsider's perspective, which may not be fully accurate.

The Call

Great characters have a need: something that drives them. It can change over time, but they have a purpose. It can be a desire, a goal, a passion, a call, a duty, an oath, a guilt, a beauty, a love, a lover, or a fear. It can be positive or negative. Write it down. Whether it be to find the six-fingered man who killed your father, or to become king of the pirates, itâ€™s good to know what you want out of life.
From a roleplaying perspective, it is best to work with the GM to ensure your goal makes sense with the planned story and group. While it is not strictly necessary for it to align, there will be more opportunities to involve your character personally in the main adventure threads if it does.

Character Traits

Choose a positive character trait. You may add +20 when a check is made in relation to roleplay to this trait.
Choose a character flaw. You may add +20 when a check is made in relation to roleplaying with this flaw.

Quirks

Eccentricity is the spice of life. No one is normal, and adventurers least of all. Donâ€™t be afraid to make your character just a bit odd. Having an interesting tick, especially early on, gives opportunities for interactions that can dynamically add to your character. If you have something early, it is much easier to change it out quickly in one of the earlier sessions

Traditional backstory and biography. Go as in depth as you desire, but please, make a two-paragraph summary for the sake of the GMâ€™s reference. The major element to focus on should be what led to them becoming an adventurer.
